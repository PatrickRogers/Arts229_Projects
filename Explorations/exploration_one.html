PROMINENCE

Jiu-Jitsu came to international prominence in the martial arts community in the early 1900’s, when Brazilian jiu jitsu (BJJ) expert Royce Gracie won the first, second, and fourth Ultimate Fighting Championships, which at the time were single elimination martial arts tournaments.

TRAINING

Sport BJJ focuses on submissions without the use of strikes while training allows practitioners to practice at full speed and with full power, resembling the effort used in a real competition. This includes technique drills, isolation sparring, and full sparring.

SAFETY

Brazilian jiu-jitsu is one of the safest martial arts. However, it is very effective as a fighter could quickly dislocate their opponents joints or knock them out with a choke-hold if they choose to. The injury rate is around 9.2-38.6 per 1000 athlete exposures.

Jiu Jitsu Is

“Upholding the premise that most of the advantage of a larger, stronger opponent comes from superior reach and powerful strikes, both of which are mitigated when grappling on the ground, Brazilian jiu-jitsu emphasizes getting an opponent to the ground in order to use ground fighting techniques and submission holds involving joint-locks and choke-holds to either win or disengage from an altercation.”
